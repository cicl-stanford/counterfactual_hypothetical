Instructions

In this experiment, your task is to make judgments about short video clips.

The full video clips will look similar to the ones shown in Figure 1:

  • In each full video clip, two balls, ball A and ball B , enter the scene from the right and collide with each other. In some of the clips, ball B ends up going through the red gate on the left (Figure 1, left), and in some of the clips ball B misses the gate (Figure 1, right).

  • There is also a brown block that may or may not move on a track. Here, in the left diagram of Figure 1, the brown block moved down from the initial position at the top; in the right diagram, the brown block stayed put throughout the video clip.

Your task:

Your task is to make a prediction about what would happen at the end of a video clip without watching it in full. The video will pause just before ball A collided with ball B (see Figure 2). You will be then asked to say to what extent do you agree with this statement:

"Ball B would go through the gate if ball A wasn't there."


Procedure:

  • We will show you a total of 10 video clips.

  • First, you will see part of the video clip twice before being asked to make your judgment; after you've made your judgment, we will show you the full video clip.

On the next slide, we will ask you a few comprehension check questions to make sure that you've understood how the experiment works.
